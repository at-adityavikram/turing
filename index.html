About Me

My name is Alan Turing, and I am a mathematician, cryptanalyst/cryptographer, and logician currently situated in the UK. With my interests spread over various disciplines and fields, I am highly passionate about subjects such as mathematics, cryptanalysis, and logic. My other interests also include philosophy, as well as mathematical biology - a field of my own creation.

I’ve also worked on redefining the concepts pertaining to continuously evolving fields such as computer science and artificial intelligence through the formalization of conventional algorithmic and computational structures.

Apart from my industrial skills and interests, I also thoroughly enjoy rowing, running (which I have performed on a professional level), as well as reading classic novels and playing and devising new board games.

Work Experience

Computer Scientist | Researcher
National Physics Laboratory, Teddington (1946-47)
I have worked as a scientist, researcher, and computer engineer at NPL, where I developed the schematics and initial design of the ACE (Automatic Computing Engine).
During my time at NPL, I also published a paper with the first detailed design of a stored-program computer.
Logician | Lead Cryptanalyst
Government Codes and Ciphers School, Bletchley Park (1939-45)
I have worked for the Government Code and Cypher School (GC&CS) at Bletchley Park, Britain's codebreaking center. I devised various cryptographical and crypto-analytical solutions and as such the machines and models for speeding the breaking of German ciphers, including a machine that could find settings for the Enigma machine.
With GC&CS, I worked on various projects pertaining to the wartime communications and encryption systems, some of them being - Bombe, the Hut8 project, Banburismus, Turingery, and Delilah.
My work is estimated to have shortened the war in Europe by more than two years and saved over 14 million lives.
Reader (Department of Mathematics)
Victoria UoM@Manchester (1948-50)
I was appointed as a Reader of the Mathematics Department at the Victoria University of Manchester.
Computer Engineer | Computational Scientist
Computing Machine Laboratory, Victoria UoM@Manchester (1948-51)
At CML, I built the world's first electronic stored-program digital computer known as the Manchester Baby, alongside founder Max Newman and co-engineers Frederic Calland Williams and Thomas Kilburn.
I was later promoted to the rank of Deputy Director of the research wing in 1949.

My Projects & Accomplishments

  • The Turing Machine
    A mathematical model of a hypothetical computing machine which can use a predefined set of rules to determine a result from a set of input variables.
  • The Turing Test
    A test developed to observe a machine's ability to exhibit intelligent behavior equivalent to, or indistinguishable from, that of a human.
  • Bombe
    An electromechanical device used to help decipher German Enigma-machine-encrypted secret messages, particularly during World War II.
  • ACE (Automatic Computing Engine)
    The early design prototype/specification of an electronic stored-program all-purpose digital computer.
